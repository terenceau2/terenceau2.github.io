  Welcome!

I am a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at University College London (UCL).

Research Interests

My research focuses on advancing techniques in named entity recognition (NER), natural language processing (NLP), and machine learning (ML). I am particularly interested in developing methods to enhance the analysis and understanding of textual data.

   Conference and invited talks

Natural Legal Language Processing (NLLP) Workshop 2022

I participated in the Natural Legal Language Processing (NLLP) Workshop, held in Abu Dhabi, UAE, in December 2022. This workshop was part of the prestigious Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP) conference. At the event, I presented a paper titled "E-NER — An Annotated Named Entity Recognition Corpus of Legal Text", co-authored with my PhD supervisor, Prof. Ingemar Cox , and Dr. Vasileios Lampos.

RUC International Conference for PhD Pioneers

In July 2023, I had the privilege of being invited as a guest speaker at the Renmin University of China, Beijing. During this event, I delivered a talk on my research focusing on low-resource Named Entity Recognition. It was an enriching opportunity to share my insights, engage with a diverse audience of scholars and students, and exchange ideas.

Symposium on Advances in Artificial Intelligence

In November 2024, I was honored to be invited as a guest speaker at the Institute of Industrial Automation and Software Engineering, University of Stuttgart. My presentation centered on biasing Named Entity Recognition models to mitigate false negative errors, showcasing techniques to enhance accuracy in challenging scenarios. This experience provided a valuable platform to present my work and to connect with a dynamic group of researchers and students.
